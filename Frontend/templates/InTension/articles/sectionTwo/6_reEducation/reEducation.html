ReEducation

Education is as much a part of the problem as it is a domain of potential solution. It fails in three ways: much of what it teaches inducts students into unsustainable ways of thought and practice; by implication, much of what it teaches comes from the past and reproduces the status quo, and is actually redundant. What it needs to do is to learn how to (i), and respond to those challenges of the present and future that reproduce unsustainable knowledge, practices and value and overcome them, and (ii) select knowledge form the past that has futural cultural value and agency, while advancing new knowledge and practices that advance sustainment.

The developmental form and contend of the education system globaly is structured toward ternary education entrance.
